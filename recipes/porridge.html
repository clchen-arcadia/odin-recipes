Porridge

Description

Chinese porridge, also known as "zhou" or "juk", is a well-loved recipe historically cooked by peasants and enjoyed by royalty alike. Today it is still a major stable: ordered at restaurants and cooked at home for a hearty breakfast or recovery meal for the sick.

The key to good porridge with a thick, silky texture, is patience. Take your time to cook our the rice, break it down, and release the starches that will make your porridge rich and taste amazing.

Ingredients

  • Uncooked rice, recommended frozen overnight
  • Chicken stock
  • Salt and white pepper powder
  • Thousand year old egg (preserved duck egg)
  • Lean chicken meat
  • Oyster sauce
  • Ginger

Steps

  1. Fill your pot with one quarter chicken stock and three quarters water and set it boiling
  2. Once your liquid is boiled, add the uncooked rice
  3. Stir as little as possible to avoid rice sticking to the bottom of your pot
  4. Cook the rice for 20 minutes and stir it once and cook until silky (an additional 20-40 minutes)
  5. You can use a whisk to speed up this process and break down the grains of rice
  6. Cut your chicken into thin strips and marinate in oyster sauce, a little potato starch, and seal together with a little oil
  7. Peel and dice your thousand year old egg
  8. Peel and mince finely (or use a grater) your ginger
  9. Once your rice porridge texture is good, add the chicken, ginger, and duck egg. And season with salt and white pepper
  10. Optionally garnish with chopped green onion
  11. Enjoy!
